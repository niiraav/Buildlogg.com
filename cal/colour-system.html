1. How Dark Mode Works — The Inversion Pattern

Buildlogg uses CSS variables that swap values between light and dark mode. The same Tailwind class (bg-status-blueBg) resolves to a light tint in light mode and a dark tint in dark mode. This is the core mechanism — any new colour must follow it.

Light = pastel wash on white. Dark = same hue, darkened to ~5-10% luminance on black. Same CSS variable, different value per mode.

2. The #0a1f3d Question — Dark Canvas Candidate

You asked about #0a1f3d (the current dark-mode blue-bg) as a potential app background. It's a deep navy — sits between the current pure black #0a0a0a and a tinted dark. Here's how it compares:

Current — #0a0a0a (pure black)
Card on #0a0a0a → #1a1a1a surface
Blue-bg banner on #0a0a0a
Nested card #262626 on #1a1a1a
Pure black. Maximum contrast. OLED-friendly. Blue-bg reads as a tinted overlay, not the canvas.
Proposed — #0a1f3d (deep navy)
Card on #0a1f3d → #0e2a52 surface
Blue-bg banner on #0a1f3d
Nested card on elevated surface
Tinted navy. Blue-bg barely visible — it's the canvas. Cards need lighter navy surfaces. Everything feels "blue".
Recommendation: Keep #0a0a0a as the dark canvas. If you switch to #0a1f3d, the blue-bg tint disappears INTO the canvas — you can't put a blue info banner on a blue background. The whole point of the tinted bg colours is that they're visible against the canvas. On pure black, #0a1f3d pops as a clear "info blue" zone. On #0a1f3d itself, it's invisible.

However — if you want a slightly tinted dark canvas (not pure black, not full navy), #0d1117 (GitHub dark) or #0f1419 (X/Twitter dark) are good middle grounds. They're dark enough that #0a1f3d still reads as blue, but warmer than pure black.

3. Replacing Jade — New Options for "Enquiry"

Jade (#0d9488) is too close to success green (#10B981). Enquiry needs a colour that reads as "new / incoming / unconfirmed" and is clearly distinct from green (Paid), blue (Booked), violet (Quoted), and amber (Awaiting Payment). Three alternatives:

Option 1 — Slate (#475569)
Enquiry Quoted Booked Awaiting Payment Paid
Slate is neutral — reads as "unprocessed, sitting in queue". Pairs with the existing --brand-dark family. Zero chromatic clash. Dark mode: #1e293b bg, #94a3b8 text.
Option 2 — Indigo (#4f46e5)
Enquiry Quoted Booked Awaiting Payment Paid
Indigo is between blue and violet — cool, professional. Distinct from both. But enquiry→quoted→booked becomes indigo→violet→blue, which is a very close hue family. Dark mode: #1e1b4b bg.
Option 3 — Rose (#e11d48) — for "new lead" energy
Enquiry Quoted Booked Awaiting Payment Paid
Rose is warm and attention-grabbing — reads as "new lead, act on this". Very distinct from every other status. Risk: could feel urgent when enquiry is just "someone called, not yet qualified". Dark mode: #1f0a0e bg, #fb7185 text. Related to DESIGN.md's badge-pink (#ec4899) but darker for readability.
Recommendation: Option 1 — Slate. It's the safest: no chromatic clash with any existing status, reads as "neutral / pending", and uses the same grey family as your brand surface tokens. Indigo (Option 2) is too close to blue+violet. Rose (Option 3) is distinctive but may feel too urgent for a simple enquiry. Slate lets the workflow colours (violet, blue, amber, green) do the talking while enquiry sits quietly as "not yet started".

Why not #0a1f3d as canvas: Your info banners, enquiry/booked badges, and blue tints all use --color-blue-bg. On pure black, #0a1f3d is clearly a blue zone. On #0a1f3d canvas, a #0a1f3d banner is invisible. You'd need a lighter navy for blue-bg, which then looks washed out. The inversion system works best when the canvas is neutral and the tints carry the hue.
